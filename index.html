  • Co-founder, Design & Frontend

What I did

Concept and layout design, UX and users flows, UI design and pixel perfect mockups, deliverable graphic assets, and later on information architecture along with HTML and CSS styling, producing guidelines and building styleguides with UI elements. I assisted my co-founders on product strategy and user data analysis.

What I learned

Co-founding a startup is like living three lives at once. Surely the most intense part of my career, I've learned the hard way that is not the number of features that makes a good product, but if you're truly addressing real users needs. At the same time, I greatly improved all my design and technical skills, jumping into code.

What I did

In charge of cover design for ZERO Magazine, I developed visual solutions for our clients, producing illustrations and supervising the printing process in case of special finishings.

Publishing a magazine every two weeks requires the ability to coordinate different jobs among different departments. When the press has started, there's no going back; I learned how to deliver fast prototypes and overcome last-minute difficulties to successfully meet tight deadlines.

  • MA Book Design & Typography
  • 2009/2010
  • University of Reading, England
What I did

History of letterforms, history of printing, ephemeral studies, layout design. I produced theoretical research and wrote essays on cognitive psychology, reading and typography; I worked on concept design, typesetting, illustration, and layout design on a series of book design briefs.

What I learned

Every typographical choice has a direct impact on the users. Typesetting and layout are simply given, in the eye of the reader; to achieve the result of a 'crystal clear' page, as a typographer I must combine grids and typefaces around the message of the text itself.

  • Jr Graphic Designer
  • 2004/2007
  • Milan–Italy
What I did

Vector illustrations and layout design; research and writing about non-linearity in communication, vision, readability and legibility; supported typeface design by drawing new glyphs for extending typset, optimizing manually kerning and hinting.

Typography is king. The route for accessing information is built with words. Typography–as both in typeface and typographical configuration–is the interface between us and the message a graphic artifact is trying to convey.
