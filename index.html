Hello, I'm Valentina and I love Bandcamp

Why me? Why Bandcamp?

You are terrific, I am terrific as well

Let me introduce myself.

I’m Valentina, a full stack designer with ten years of experience in the field. Right now I work as an independent product designer, building websites and web apps from static mock-ups to their implementation. I believe in design as a problem-solving process, where creativity is the ability to find simple answers to complex issues. I agree that "cultural awareness is the most important quality of the good designer”. [cit.]

Researching, sketching, mock-upping, coding, testing: I’m convinced iteration is the key to sustainable design solutions. Sublime Text is my best friend, I know my way with Git and the terminal. HTML, CSS, and Stylus preprocessing are my everyday tools; I love–and loath!–CSS to the point of writing my own framework.

When I’m not lost in the editor, I’m playing around with vectors or experimenting with drawing styles.

Music means the world to me. So Bandcamp.

I am a music nerd. I grew up in a depressed area of Italy, where a free independent radio run by guys and girls from the neighbourhood–on a voluntary basis–was the only way to escape the post-industrial gloom. I identify with the DIY community, I go to gigs on a weekly basis and I write She Does Scream, a blog on female frontend bands, that me and my friend Dana will soon turn into a rad music label.

The moment I signed up for Bandcamp, it completely changed the way I listen, consume, buy and discover music. Free streaming, ‘name your price’, redeem codes: I was stoked. I admire how Bandcamp is empowering both fans and musicians, letting the audience directly supports the artists they love.
